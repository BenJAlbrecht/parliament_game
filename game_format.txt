PARLIAMENT GAME - FORMAT BRAINSTORM
=====================================

CORE CONCEPT
You lead a party, build a coalition, and survive a term.

GAME LOOP
Each term you face ~10 bills. On each bill you declare your party's stance
(aye/nay), your coalition partners mostly follow, and opposition parties push
back. At the end of the term there's an election — your score is driven by:
  - How many bills you passed that aligned with your party's ideology
  - How stable your coalition was
  - Your approval rating

Then seats shift based on performance and you start the next term. Repeat
until you lose power.

PER-TURN LOOP
  pick stance → watch vote → coalition drifts or holds → election → new term with shifted seats

CORE TENSION
Passing centrist bills keeps your coalition intact but erodes your base.
Playing to your base risks the coalition collapsing.

ALTERNATIVE CONSIDERED
Opposition mode — you try to block the government rather than govern.
Rejected: easier to balance but less satisfying since you're always reacting,
never governing.
