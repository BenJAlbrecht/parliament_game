PARLIAMENT GAME - FORMAT & DESIGN
===================================

CORE CONCEPT
You lead a party, build a coalition, and survive a term. Each turn you propose
a bill. Bills that are far from your coalition partners' ideologies erode their
loyalty — and a partner that falls to 0% loyalty leaves the coalition,
potentially collapsing your government.

CORE TENSION
Every bill is a negotiation between ambition and survival. Pushing controversial
bills gets things done but costs you coalition health. Playing it safe keeps
partners stable but may leave your agenda unfinished.


IDEOLOGY AXES
Each party has two scores, each from -10 to +10:
  economic  (-10 = fully state-led / collectivist, +10 = fully free market)
  social    (-10 = fully progressive / libertarian, +10 = fully traditional / authoritarian)

Current party positions:
  People's Alliance   econ: -9   social: -7
  Social Democrats    econ: -4   social: -4
  Renewal             econ: +2   social: -5
  Christian Democrats econ: +4   social: +5
  National Front      econ: +1   social: +9


BILLS
Each bill has:
  title   — display name
  type    — 'economic' or 'social' (which axis it acts on)
  score   — position on that axis, -10 to +10

The bill's score is compared against each coalition partner's value on the
same axis to compute ideological distance (IDIS, k).


VOTE SUPPORT FORMULA (VUF)
The fraction of a partner's MPs who vote Aye is:

  k   = | bill.score - partner[bill.type] |     // ideology distance, 0–20
  c   = max(0, 1 - k/10)                        // ideology support, 0–1
  L   = loyalty / 100                           // normalised loyalty, 0–1

  VUF = L + (1 - L) × c

Interpretation:
  - At L=1 (full loyalty): VUF = 1. Full coalition discipline — partner votes
    Aye on everything regardless of ideology.
  - At L=0 (zero loyalty): VUF = c. Partner votes purely by their own ideology.
  - At k=0 (perfect alignment): VUF = 1 at any loyalty level.
  - At k≥10 (strong opposition): c=0, so VUF = L. Only discipline drives votes.

  ayes from partner = floor(VUF × partner.seats)


COALITION LOYALTY
Each coalition partner starts at 100% loyalty.
Loyalty is tracked per partner and persists across the session.

When a bill PASSES, loyalty changes for each partner:

  distance     = | bill.score - partner[bill.type] |   // 0–20 range
  loyaltyDelta = max(-20, min(5, 5 - distance × 1.5))

  Examples:
    distance 0   → +5    (perfect alignment — loyalty grows)
    distance 3.3 → 0     (breakeven — no change)
    distance 5   → -2.5
    distance 10  → -10
    distance 15  → -17.5 → clamped to -20
    distance 17  → -20   (capped)

When a bill FAILS, no loyalty changes (wasted turn).

If a partner's loyalty reaches 0%, they leave the coalition. If the coalition
loses its majority as a result, the government falls.


PER-TURN LOOP
  1. Player is dealt 3 random bills to choose from
  2. Player picks one to propose
  3. Vote resolves — each coalition partner contributes floor(VUF × seats) Ayes;
     opposition votes entirely Nay; player's own party always votes Aye
  4. If passed: loyalty updates per partner (delta = 5 - distance × 1.5, clamped)
  5. If failed: wasted turn, no loyalty change
  6. Repeat for 10 bills per term

After 10 bills: session ends. Win condition TBD.


COALITION STRUCTURE
Three pre-set coalition blocs, each with a narrative scenario:
  Left Bloc       — People's Alliance + Social Democrats + Renewal  (251 seats)
  Grand Coalition — Social Democrats + Christian Democrats           (278 seats)
  Right Bloc      — Renewal + Christian Democrats + National Front  (298 seats)

Parties with multiple options (SD, Renewal, CD) choose their coalition on
Page 3 before the game begins. Coalition loyalty starts at 100% for all
partners regardless of coalition chosen.

Total seats: 466. Majority: 234.
